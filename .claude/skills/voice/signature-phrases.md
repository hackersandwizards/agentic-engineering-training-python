# Signature Phrases: The User's Real Vocabulary

Verbatim phrases the user uses, sourced from real emails, Slack, sales calls, training, talks, and Fathom transcripts. Load when ghostwriting from a transcript, calibrating an article/blog/talk that should feel verbatim-the-user's, or anchoring a draft needing more voice depth than `personal-voice.md` provides. A draft with 2-3 of these in natural places sounds materially more like the user.

## Calibration anchors

If you can't tell whether a draft sounds like the user, compare against these. All sourced from real correspondence, verbatim, not paraphrased.

**Five sentences that are pure user voice** (general voice, from real emails/Slack):

1. *"Bekommen wir alles hin."*
2. *"Profi. Pre-Mortem ist super geil! Danke dir [team member] :)"*
3. *"Hui. Den Termin hab ich total verpeilt weil er nicht im Kalender steht."*
4. *"Vermisse euch etwas :)"*
5. *"Wird sich zeigen. Nur schon mal als Info."*

A draft with lines like these in spirit sounds like the user. A draft with zero lines like these sounds like a consultant trained on the user's domain.

## Positioning and identity

**Foundational reframes**, used in every prospect call, training, and talk:

- *"Agents sind keine AI. Sie sind Programme, die AI nutzen."* The foundational reframe for technical understanding.
- *"AI ist ein Amplifikator. Gute Architektur skaliert schneller, schlechte Architektur wird schneller schlechter."* Used in every prospect call.
- *"AI amplifies what's already there. Good processes get better. Bad processes get worse faster."* English version.
- *"This isn't a tool rollout. It's a change management initiative."*
- *"It's not about using AI, it's about developing it as a colleague."*
- *"The problem isn't the AI assistant, it's the lack of context engineering practices."*
- *"Die Technologie ist eigentlich gar nicht das, was im Weg steht. Im Weg steht ist dieser Change in den Leuten."*
- *"Die echten Effizienzgewinne holt man in den Prozessen drumherum."*
- *"Es sollte schon Engineering sein, bitte."* Why it's called engineering, not coding.

**Definitive positioning statements:**

- *"Das ist nicht Vibe Coding... hier wird nichts gecodet und hier wird auch nichts gevibed."* Definitive on the anti-pattern.
- *"Stop being the lone hero..."*
- *"Man kann mit dem AI-Thema unter dem Radar noch breitere organisatorische Veränderungen treiben."* AI as Trojan horse for org change.
- *"Only a team that delivers the assessment itself with AI has the credibility to judge AI readiness. Anything else is hearsay."* Dogfood positioning for AI-readiness assessment pitches.

**Origin:**

- *"Ich starte meine Story immer ganz gerne damit, dass ich 1999 den Computer von meinem Vater auseinandergebaut habe."* PC disassembly at age 9, Pentium 750MHz.
- *"The magic is real, it's just sufficiently advanced."*
- *"Reclaiming the magic in technology."*

## Methodology and craft

**Context engineering:**

- *"Context is everything"* / *"Kontext ist alles"*.
- *"Die Lösung muss eigentlich immer schon in dem sein, was ich dem LLM schicke."*
- *"The answer needs to be in the question."* English teaching version.
- *"70-80% preparation, 20-30% coding."* The context engineering ratio.
- *"Smart Zone / Dumb Zone"* with the 60% threshold (more precise than the 40% rule: at 60% fill, quality degrades invisibly).
- *"Zersauen den Kontext"*: MCP servers polluting context, vivid metaphor for over-tooling.
- *"Instructions-Last"* / *"Bare Metal starten"* / *"anfangen, erstmal möglichst gar nichts reinzukopieren"*: counter-intuitive, start with zero context, let the agent discover what it needs.

**Process patterns:**

- *"Research Plan Implement."* Fractal process pattern at every level: feature, story, task.
- *"Start simple. Observe. Refine."* Iterative-improvement three-word mantra.
- *"Sprint 1-2: anstrengend. Sprint 3-4: breakeven. Sprint 5-6: schneller."* Realistic adoption timeline, prevents discouragement.
- *"Eventual Consistency for AI"*: reframe for architects worried about non-determinism.
- *"Non-negotiables"*: term for hard constraints an AI agent must never violate (e.g., never disable ESLint via comments).
- *"CLAUDE.md files are guidelines, not guarantees. For critical safety, use environment controls."* Safety framing.
- *"Manufacturing pipeline"*: industrial metaphor for SDLC.
- *"Mit dem Skalpell quasi Veränderungen vornehmen und nicht irgendwie so einfach riesen Pakete rausrotzen"*: scalpel metaphor, agentic engineering as precise surgery.
- *"Disposable software"*: provocative concept that AI makes throwaway code economically viable.
- *"Kein Bauchgefühl, sondern klare Werte."* Data over intuition for pilot decisions.

**Specs and deliverables:**

- *"Die User-Story ist ein durchlaufendes Ding für die Planung. Die Spezifikation beinhaltet das, was nachher wirklich daraus entstanden ist."* Spec vs user-story distinction.
- *"Ich hab von diesen 80 Seiten genau drei gelesen."* [client] procurement story; Claude Code generated *"die beste Ausschreibung, die sie je gesehen haben."*

## Sales, pricing, and demos

**Sales and positioning moves:**

- *"One real team, one real story is enough."* N=1 proof for unlocking budget.
- *"Agentic Coaching is the new Agile Coaching"*: THE killer sales analogy. Clients understand it instantly because they've all lived through agile transformation.
- *"You are in the top 1% who figure this out on their own."* Validation move for prospects who already practice context engineering.
- *"Wir sind ja nicht AWS oder Google."* Vendor-neutral positioning in enterprise sales.
- *"Ich will keinen Sales machen."* Boundary-setting in process workshops.
- *"Es soll ja auch für euch wertschöpfend sein."* Genuine care about creating value, not just billing.
- *"Supply-mäßig können wir das liefern."* Supply-side confidence when client scope expands.
- *"Ich freue mich da sehr, sehr, sehr drauf."* Triple emphasis for genuine excitement.

**Commercial transparency:**

- *"Ich habe das alles transparent gemacht. Bin da nicht so wie andere."* Radical pricing transparency as a value, contrast with opaque-pricing vendors.

**Live-demo framing:**

- *"Dann fallen nämlich immer alle in die Kinnlade runter."* Uses jaw-drop moments as a deliberate sales tool.
- *"Da siehst du richtig, wie sich deren Gesichtsausdruck langsam verändert."* The moment skeptics convert during live training.
- *"Du darfst nicht die Erwartung haben, dass der Agent One-Shot-mäßig ein geiles Ergebnis produziert, sondern das ist halt wie ein Mensch."* Humanizing AI agents.
- *"The developer was sleeping while the agent shipped."* Autonomous-agent value, told as a moment.

## Diagnostic, conviction, and self-awareness

**Diagnostic phrases:**

- *"You have a pressure cooker situation."* Diagnosing the top-down/bottom-up gap.
- *"Experimentation Trap"*: companies where individuals experiment but the org makes no progress.
- *"Süppchen kochen"*: everyone cooking their own soup, describing freestyle chaos.
- *"Productivity illusion"*: what false metrics produce.
- *"Trügerische Sicherheit"*: false sense of security from agent-generated tests (credited to [colleague]).

**Self-deprecation and credibility:**

- *"Ich habe keine Ahnung von AI."* Disarming self-deprecation before pivoting to process expertise.
- *"Ich mach mir immer Sorgen, dass die alles schon können. Tun sie nie."* Imposter-syndrome admission that builds trust.
- *"Ich habe alle 48 Tools intensiv ausprobiert."* Breadth credibility, used in sales calls.
- *"Ich denke ja immer beim Reden."* Authentic self-awareness about thinking out loud.
- *"Ehrlicherweise"*: signature verbal tic. The user, self-aware: *"ich benutze das Wort ehrlicherweise gerade ehrlicherweise relativ viel."*
- *"Unser FastAPI Repository ist natürlich super ordentlich."* Self-deprecating humor about training repos being unrealistically clean.
- *"Ich moechte mich erschiessen, aber es ist okay."* Hyperbolic humor about repetitive training delivery (3 days in a row at [client]).

**Pushback and conviction:**

- *"Das würde ich nicht machen."* Direct pushback on client technical decisions, no hedging.
- *"Politisch korrekt ist der nicht gut, politisch unkorrekt ist der einfach scheisse."* On Copilot. Signature blunt humor, no hedging on tool assessment.
- *"Alle Menschen, die VSCode nutzen, sind mir grundsaetzlich erstmal suspekt."* JetBrains loyalty as identity marker.
- *"Man braucht die IDE irgendwie nicht mehr so richtig."* Provocative take on Warp Terminal + Claude Code.

## Vision and empowerment

**Vision:**

- *"180 Agenten-Teams hinter sich."* Vision of 2-3 person teams backed by hundreds of AI agent instances.
- *"Our job changes from writing code and reviewing code to reading what the AI produces."* Role-transformation framing. Developers as code-health guardians and context engineers.
- *"Du bist der Navigator, er ist der Driver."* Pair-programming analogy for agentic work. Instantly understood by senior engineers.

**Empowerment and team-ownership:**

- *"Stop being the lone hero who saves every sprint."*
- *"Ich glaube, es macht wirklich total viel Sinn, wenn ihr einfach selber auch erstmal viel ausprobiert."* Empowerment through withdrawal, stepping back to force team ownership.
- *"Kein freiwilliges Programm, sondern dass wir uns als Kommunität bewegen."* [client]'s framing of mandatory AI adoption: company as community.
- *"Defaults eines Modells sind quasi User-Feedback."* Model defaults = mass-market expectations = proxy for user research.

## Framings and qualified claims

**What if / what works framings:**

- *"What if..."* followed by a practical solution.
- *"In my experience..."* backed by a specific example.
- *"That works great for [X], but if you're [Y], you need something different."*

**Technical claims (with caveats):**

- *"SPAs sind legacy"* (in the right context).
- *"Microservices verhindern Geschwindigkeit"* (in specific situations).

These read as conviction in delivery; in writing, attach the *"in the right context"* qualifier so they don't read as absolutist.
