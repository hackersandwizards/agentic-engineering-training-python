# Pitch Framework: CAPSTONE

Eight-beat scaffold for cold pitches, proposals, talk openers, and sales arcs. Adapted from Daniel Priestley's Key Person of Influence methodology. Skip for in-progress threads where the recipient already has context.

## C: Clarity, what they get

Open with the outcome they receive, not who you are.

- Their benefit in under 10 words.
- Tangible and specific.
- Test: would a 10-year-old understand and be excited?

> *"Your developers become confident AI practitioners. Your velocity goes up while quality improves. Your team stays fulfilled."*

## A: Authority, stories that prove

One specific success story that demonstrates capability.

- One client, one number, one moment.
- Recent and relevant.
- The story is the credential. Skip titles and degrees.

> *"Last month an [client] dev told me: 'Our 3-week sprints became 1-week sprints, and our developers are more fulfilled than ever.' Context engineering changed how they build software."*

## P: Problem, their 3am worry

Name the fear they haven't voiced, the real problem beneath the symptoms.

- Go deeper than surface issues.
- Address the emotional and the business pain.
- Test: do they think *"How did you know?"* If not, go deeper.

> *"Your developers tried AI assistants and got frustrated. Half disabled them; the other half are shipping code that makes architects cringe. You're carrying scattered AI adoption while the technical debt from generative AI keeps growing."*

## S: Solution, show the magic happening

Let them see the shift in action, not the framework slides.

- Show a moment of success they can visualize.
- The experience, not the features.
- Help them see themselves succeeding.

> *"Watch what happens when your developers become confident AI practitioners through context engineering, instead of fighting vibe coding."*

## T: The Why, your breaking point

Share the personal moment that created your obsession with solving this.

- Real story of frustration or realization.
- Personal and specific.
- Connect your pain to their gain.

> *"I worked with brilliant developers losing their programming joy to scattered AI adoption and vibe coding. That's when I realized: the problem isn't AI assistants. It's the missing agentic engineering practices."*

## O: Opportunity, paint their success scene

One vivid image of their future.

- A specific scene they can feel.
- Emotional and practical benefits together.
- Make them want that Monday morning.

> *"Imagine your next standup: 'We shipped three features this week. Our developers are confident AI practitioners. The team is more fulfilled than ever.'"*

## N: Next steps, one clear door

A single, specific action they can take immediately.

- One option. Confusion kills action.
- Low commitment, high value.
- Specific about time and format.

> *"Twenty-minute coffee chat about your biggest AI frustration. Book here: [link]"*

## E: Essence, energy forward

Leave them with momentum, not a summary.

- End with possibility, not recap.
- Forward motion, not closure.
- Make them feel the potential.

> *"The magic is real. It's just been buried under bad practices. Let's dig it out together."*

## CAPSTONE quickcard

```
C: Their win in 10 words
A: One "holy shit" success story
P: Their 3am worry
S: Show the magic happening
T: Your breaking point story
O: Their Monday morning smile
N: One click to start
E: Energy forward, always
```
